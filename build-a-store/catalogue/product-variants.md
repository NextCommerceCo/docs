# Product Variants

## Understanding Product **Variants**

If your product has multiple sizes, colors, or other variations, you can create variants from the Product Details page.  For example, a T-shirt might be offered in multiple sizes and colors.  Each combination of color and size would correspond to a specific product variant.

{% hint style="info" %}
Product Variants each have a corresponding Product ID, and require their own respective unique SKU code. &#x20;
{% endhint %}

If your product has multiple variants you should think of them as separate products, in that they may have unique product details (including storefront styling, attributes, SKU and so on).

## **Create Product Variants**

Create product variants from the Product Details View.

To convert an existing stand-alone product to a variant, select the **More Actions** menu, and choose **Convert to Variant.**

For products with existing variants, simply choose **Add Variant Product** from the **Product Variants** card.

### Create Product Variant Attributes

Variants are defined by the specific attributes available to them. These attributes can be set up and assigned from the Product Details View, on the **Product Variants** tab, by selecting **Add Variant Attribute.**

To use the example of a T-shirt that comes in two sizes (small and large) and three colors (red, green, blue), you would define these **variant attributes** when adding the product to your store.

Select **Add Variant Attribute**, and create two variant attributes: Color & Size

* To set up the Color product attribute&#x20;
  * Set the Name field to “Color”
  * Set the “Option” field to "Red"
  * Choose **Add Variant Attribute** to define additional Option fields for "Green" and "Blue"
* Click **Save**

{% hint style="info" %}
To add additional variant product attributes, such as Size, click the **Add Variant Attribute** button on the **Product Variants** card, and repeat the above steps, then click **Save**.&#x20;
{% endhint %}

From here, you can continue to set up Variant products in the same way as a stand-alone product. Remember, variant products have their own respective Product IDs and SKU codes.
